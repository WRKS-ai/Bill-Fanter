High-volatility markets are where trading stories come from — both kinds. The account-doubling week and the account-ending week usually happen in the same environment. That's not a coincidence: volatility amplifies everything, and options amplify it again.

In the video above I show what this looks like on real charts. Here's what actually changes when the market gets wild, and how I adjust.

## What high volatility does to options

Three things happen at once, and each one changes your trade math:

### 1. Premiums inflate

Options are priced on expected movement. When the market expects chaos, every contract gets more expensive. The call that cost $2.00 in a calm market might cost $5.00 in a storm — same stock, same strike. If you buy it, you now need a much bigger move just to break even.

### 2. Ranges explode

Stocks that moved 1% a day start moving 3–5%. Your winners can run further than usual — but your losers hit harder and faster too. Price can blow through levels that held for months, and gaps can jump right over your stop.

### 3. Everything speeds up

Decisions that used to take an afternoon now take minutes. Hesitation gets expensive, and so does panic. Fast markets punish traders who haven't decided in advance what they'll do.

## Why this creates huge wins

Volatility is opportunity — that part of the story is true. Directional trades that catch a big move pay multiples of what they'd pay in a quiet market. And for premium *sellers*, inflated options mean you're being paid unusually well for taking the other side. Some of the best setups of the year happen when fear is at its peak.

## Why it creates huge losses

The same leverage, in reverse. Traders who keep their calm-market habits in a wild market get hurt in predictable ways:

- **Full-size positions in triple-size ranges.** If the daily range triples and your size doesn't change, your risk just tripled. Most blown-up accounts trace back to this one line.
- **Buying expensive premium late.** Chasing a move after volatility has spiked means paying peak prices for options that lose value fast when things calm down — even if the direction was right.
- **Trading every candle.** Big moves trigger big emotions. More trades, less selectivity, worse results. Volatile markets reward patience and punish activity.

## How I adjust

My rules in high-volatility markets are simple, and they're all decided before the open:

1. **Cut position size.** When ranges expand, size comes down so the dollar risk per trade stays constant. This is non-negotiable.
2. **Demand better setups.** Fewer trades, higher standards. In a fast market, a mediocre setup is a donation.
3. **Respect the premium.** Check what you're paying. When implied volatility is extreme, I lean toward strategies that benefit from it rather than fight it. (If you're new to reading volatility, start with my [VIX explainer](/blog/what-is-the-vix).)
4. **Know the worst case in dollars.** Not percentages — dollars. If that number keeps you up at night, the position is too big.
